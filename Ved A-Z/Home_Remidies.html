Common Cold

Common Cold is relatively harmless condition. It can occur at any time throughout the year but the most common are in late winter and fall. The cases generally occur among the children. But if ignored can acts as a gateway to many other respiratory problems. Some of its symptoms are- • Running or blocking of nose • Scratchy throat • Cough • Headache • Mild fever with chills Homemade remdies- • Leaves of holy basil (Tulsi) plant mixed with an equal quantity of dried ginger powder. This may be taken by adding milk and sugar three times a day. • Juice of ginger (Adrak) and holy basil (Tulsi) one-one tea spoon (5 ml.) mixed with one tea spoon (5 ml.) honey, to be taken two-three times a day. • Powder of black pepper (marich) half tea spoon (2.5 ml.) mixed with jaggery (guda) two times a day. • Small piece of Ginger (Adraka) fried in ghee, to be taken two times a day. • One teaspoonful of the powder or paste of turmeric to a cup of milk or fried turmeric powder taken with 1-2 gm of ghee mixed with hot milk thrice a day. • Gargle with warm turmeric powder 2-3 times a day to relieve sore throat. • Try to keep drinking warm water whole day. It will bring ease to throat and our body too. • Take steams of hot water for minimum 10 minutes. • Sooth the throat and relieve cough with a home remedy such as lemon with honey, ginger, tulsi, warm water etc.

    PREVENTIONS/Self-care-
  • Keep away from others who are infected with cold.
  • Eat a well-balanced diet and get enough rest. Take plenty of hot beverages.
  • For new born babies, apply above remedies in small doses.
  • Do not over heat the room. Let the body lose heat naturally.

3. Obesity:-

Obesity is the accumulation of excess fat in the body which may lead to negative effects on health, reduced life expectancy and/or increased health problems. It is considered as one of Santarpanottha Vikaras (disease due to consumption of excessive calories) in Ayurveda. Medodushti (disorders of fat metabolism) may be one of the risk factors for ischemic heart disease (IHD). Homemade remedies- • 5gm of Triphala powder of this powder (1 tea spoon) should be taken with luke warm water at bed time. • The 4-6 tea spoon (20-30 ml.) of castor oil is to be taken with warm milk. But the caster oil should be purified and diluted. • 1 Teaspoonful of Sana leaves (Sanay) in 1 cup of hot milk at bedtime is another easy method to relieve constipation.

    Preventions-
  • High carbohydrate vegetables like - potato, rice etc.
  • More sugary or sweet products, more dairy products, fried and oily foods, fast foods, excess salt.
  • Salty foods or excessive salt in meals.

4.Good Digestion:-

Some people try to attain a good and healthy life style by the means of good appetite or a heathy diet. Some the suggestions or remedies are- • Eat three meals a day. • A light breakfast, a substantial lunch and a light dinner allow you to keep in tune with the ebb and flow of the digestive fire, which builds up during the morning, peaks around noon and then ebbs again in the evening. • The ideal ayurvedic lunch includes two or three kinds of vegetables, one of which should be a leafy green; a lentil or bean dish or a paneer dish; a whole grain; a chutney or relish; a small helping of a warm salad or soup; and lassi, a beverage made by blending together fresh yogurt and water. • Drink lots of pure water through the day, but limit your water or beverage intake at meals. Do not drink iced, carbonated or caffeinated beverages and avoid alcohol and milk with meals.

    Preventions-
  • Avoid incompatible food combinations. Ayurvedic texts outline some food combinations that overtax the digestion and lead to increased ama build-up.
  • Milk and cream, for example, should not be combined with salty or sour tastes.
  • Melons should not be eaten with heavy foods like cheese, deep fried foods or the heavier grains.
  • Meat or fish should not be taken together with milk.
  • Honey should never be heated or cooked.

5.Dry Cough:-

Dry cough (Shushka-Kasa) is a very common ailment especially in cities. The allergens or pollutants in the environment enter your throat pipe, lungs or airways and trigger your body to cough them out, redulting in a very hash and rough condition of our throat. Its ignorance can cause inflammation of the respiratory tract and throat. Dry cough can last for a very long time, even become chronic. The main causes of dry cough are respiratory tract infections, Sinusitis, Pneumonia, suppression of vomiting and inflammation due to irritants/allergens. Remedies- • take warm milk with turmeric and honey. • Consume turmeric powder mixed with honey three times a day. • Licorice (Mulethi) lozenges to reduce inflammation • Sooth your infected throat pipe with natural cough syrup made by mixing ginger and honey. Boil a piece of ginger (peeled) in water. Filter and mix 1 tsp of honey and consume. You can even chew small pieces of ginger sprinkled with water for its anti-inflammatory properties. • Add a pinch of cinnamon powder in warm water or tea for relief from throat irritability • Drink tea made of Tulsi (Basil leaves), ginger and honey

    Preventions-
  • Avoid interaction with allergens (pollution, dust, pollen etc.)
  • Abstain from junk, dry or spicy food
  • A nourishing diet should be taken in small quantities (preferably as soups) until the digestive Agni becomes strong
  • Have plenty of rest
  • Do Pranayama for building lungs health
  • Avoid irritants like smoke from mosquito coils, incense etc
  • Follow strict food and sleep schedule. Have dinner before sunset
  • Install humidifier in the bedroom
